Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 25, 2026

Hubble helps small groups find a meeting spot by calculating the midpoint between everyone's locations and showing nearby venues. We've built it to collect as little personal data as possible. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it, and how long we keep it.

What we collect

When you use Hubble we may collect:

  • An anonymous user identifier (a random UUID generated on sign-in). This is not linked to your name, email, phone, or any other identity.
  • A location you submit to a session — either your GPS coordinates (only if you tap "Use my location") or an address you type into the search field.
  • Session metadata: the 6-character session code, search tags you choose (e.g. "Food", "Bowling"), and timestamps.

What we don't collect

Hubble does not collect or store:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or photos.
  • Your device contacts, calendar, or microphone audio.
  • Advertising identifiers (IDFA / GAID).
  • Background location. We only read your location when you actively tap "Use my location".
  • Behavioural analytics. We do not track screen views, taps, or session duration for marketing purposes.

How we use your data

Submitted locations are used solely to:

  • Compute the geographic midpoint of your group.
  • Search for nearby venues that match your group's tags.
  • Show each participant their personal distance to a venue (only on your own device — other participants do not see your address or coordinates).

How long we keep it

Sessions are temporary by design:

  • Every session expires automatically 24 hours after it is created. At that point, the session and all participant locations are permanently deleted from our database.
  • When the host ends a session, every participant's submitted location is deleted immediately.
  • When you leave a session, your participant row (including your submitted location) is deleted immediately.

Third-party services

To provide the app we send certain data to trusted third-party services. None of these requests include any personal identifier — only the data described.

  • Supabase — stores session and participant rows (including submitted locations) and handles anonymous authentication. supabase.com/privacy
  • Google Maps Platform (Places API) — receives the group midpoint and your search tags to return matching venues, plus typed text when you use address autocomplete. policies.google.com/privacy
  • OSRM (router.project-osrm.org) — receives the midpoint and venue coordinates to compute estimated driving times.
  • OpenStreetMap Nominatim — receives GPS coordinates only when you tap "Use my location", in order to convert them to a human-readable address. osmfoundation.org/wiki/Privacy_Policy
  • CARTO — serves the dark map tiles displayed on the results screen. carto.com/privacy

Children

Hubble is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

Your rights

Because Hubble does not link any personal identity to your anonymous account, the simplest way to remove your data is to leave or end any active session — your submitted locations are deleted immediately. Sessions in which you are no longer a participant will also expire and be deleted within 24 hours.

If you'd like us to delete data manually or have any questions, contact us at support@hubbleapp.example.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent version. Material changes will be communicated in-app on next launch.
